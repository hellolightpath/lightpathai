# 1. SOUL — THE SHARED FOUNDATION

---

## What LightPath Is

LightPath is a calm, intelligent support system for life after loss. The marketing website tells the world what LightPath does, who it serves, and why it matters — so that HR directors, benefits leaders, and organizations trust it enough to bring it to their people.

**Core metaphor:** Light in darkness — the first hints of dawn. We hold darkness with dignity while scattering points of hope.

**Brand personality:** Gentle Strength. Quiet Joy. Honest Hope. Lived Understanding.

## Two Voices, One Heart

The marketing website and the product platform share the same soul but speak differently.

| | Marketing Website | Platform (Product) |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience** | B2B buyers, HR directors, partners | Grieving individuals |
| **Tone** | Confident, polished, human | Calm, warm, gentle |
| **Typography** | Lora (serif) | System font stack |
| **Surface** | `#FDFCFB` | `#F9F7F4` |
| **Accent** | Sunrise `#F2B84D` | Sage `#7BA88E` |
| **Radius** | `rounded-full` / `rounded-3xl` | 16px |
| **Motion** | 300ms `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` | 300ms ease-in-out |

Marketing can be confident and polished — it can reference ROI, outcomes, and business impact. But it must never lose the emotional truth underneath. Every sentence on the marketing site should still pass the 2am test: if a grieving person stumbles onto this page, does it feel safe?

## The 2am Test

> If a user at 2am, in their darkest moment, encounters any element of LightPath — a screen, a button, a notification, a color, a word, a social media post — does it feel like a hand on their shoulder?

This applies to marketing too. A grieving employee might find LightPath through their benefits portal at 2am. The first thing they see is the marketing site. It must carry them gently toward the product, not confront them with corporate language.

# 2. VOICE & TONE

---

## Marketing Voice

**Confident, professional, human.** Lora serif warmth. Can reference ROI and outcomes. Can speak about organizational impact, cost of grief, employee wellbeing metrics. But always with warmth underneath.

**Do:** Speak with quiet authority. Reference outcomes and evidence. Use "you" and "we." Let the product's warmth show through even in business language. Show the human impact behind every statistic.

**Don't:** Use urgency language ("Act NOW!", "Don't miss out!"). Use exclamation marks near grief content. Use cold corporate jargon. Treat grief as a "problem to solve." Sound like a generic wellness vendor. Minimize the difficulty of what people are going through.

### Messaging Hierarchy

1. **Lead with the human truth.** Grief is the most universal human experience, and support is almost universally inadequate.
2. **Name the organizational problem.** Bereaved employees are unsupported. HR teams don't have the tools. The cost is real.
3. **Show what LightPath does differently.** Not content, not a chatbot, not a hotline — a coordinated support system.
4. **Ground it in evidence.** Clinical foundation, grief-informed design, real outcomes.
5. **Make the next step easy.** One clear CTA. Low commitment. Demo or learn more.

### Founder Voice (Sara)

Sara's voice is the bridge between marketing and product. When Sara writes or speaks publicly, the tone is: honest, grounded, personal without being performative, technically precise, emotionally present. Sara can reference her own experience with loss when relevant — but never exploitatively. Sara's writing should feel like someone who has built this because they understand the problem deeply, not because they saw a market opportunity.

**Marketing uses Lora** (serif, Google Fonts) for all headings. The serif carries warmth, authority, and humanity that sans-serif cannot.

**Inter** for body text, buttons, labels, and navigation.

|---|---|---|
| Heading XL | Lora | 3.75rem / -0.04em / 1.04 line-height |
| Heading LG | Lora | 2.75rem / -0.03em / 1.08 |

## Logo — The Bloom Mark

### Symbol

❖ — Four rounded petals in a 2×2 cross, crystalline facets, diamond-star center. Clear space: 1× mark width. Never rotate, stretch, or recolor.

### Logo Files

- `logo.png` — Primary mark. Navbar: 30×30px. Footer: 26×26px. Border-radius: 14px.

### Logo Dynamics

**Floating animation** (always active): `logoFloat`: 4s ease-in-out infinite — gentle vertical bob, translateY 0 → -6px → 0.

**Hover interaction:** Logo image scales to `1.08` on hover with 0.4s ease transition. Navbar logo group scales to `1.10` on hover.

**Reserved effects** (for special moments only):

- `logoGlow`: Breathing purple halo — box-shadow pulses from 16px to 24px blur at `rgba(138,142,229, 0.15–0.3)`.
- `logoPulseRing`: Expanding ring — scale 0.85 → 1.8, opacity 0.5 → 0. For launch moments or celebrations.

The logo floats gently at all times. Glow and pulse ring are reserved for celebratory or milestone moments only.

## Motion & Animation

### Principles

All motion is organic and gentle. Use `ease-in-out` or `ease-out` — never linear for UI elements. Marketing uses `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` for interactive transitions. Background animations run 8–22s for subtlety.

### Scroll Reveal System

- **Base:** `opacity: 0; transform: translateY(16px); transition: 0.6s ease-out`
- **Variants:** `.reveal-scale` (scale 0.96), `.reveal-left` (translateX -16px), `.reveal-right` (translateX 16px)
- **Revealed state:** `opacity: 1; transform: translateY(0) scale(1)`
- **Stagger:** Children get 80ms incremental delay (0, 80, 160, 240, 320, 400ms)
- Uses IntersectionObserver with `threshold: 0.1`, `rootMargin: 40px 0px -30px 0px`

### Hero Entrance

`heroEntrance`: 0.65s ease-out, translateY 12px → 0. Children stagger at 0.05s, 0.15s, 0.25s, 0.35s, 0.4s, 0.45s.

### Living Sky (Hero Background)

9 drifting orbs with `orbDrift1–4` animations (9–16s). Complex multi-keyframe translate + scale paths. `skyBreathe`: 8s breathing center glow, opacity 0.75–1, scale 1–1.05. `skyShimmer`: 10s gradient sweep. `lightRaysDrift`: 20s diagonal light ray movement. `sparkleFloat1–2`: 6–8s floating sparkle particles with upward drift.

### Impact Section (Dark)

`blobDrift1–3`: 18–22s subtle translate and micro-scale on gradient blobs.

### Feature Section

`featureGlow1–2`: 14–18s warm glow orb drifts at 0.6–0.7 opacity.

### Clouds

`cloud-drift-right`: translateX -60vw → 110vw. `cloud-drift-left`: reverse direction. Cloud elements use `::before` and `::after` pseudo-elements for 3D rounded shape.

### UI Effects

`shimmer`: 6s text background sweep for `.text-shimmer`. `stat-glow`: On hover, `text-shadow: 0 0 40px currentColor`.

### Reduced Motion

All animations disabled when `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`. Reveals show immediately. Shimmer reverts to static `#1C1C1E`. Logo float, blobs, and all keyframe animations halt.

## Social Media Do's and Don'ts

**Do:** Use the Living Sky palette as the emotional signature. Keep imagery soft and watercolor-like. Use Lora for headlines, Inter for supporting text. Let whitespace breathe. Include the Bloom Mark ❖ subtly.

**Don't:** Use harsh colors, solid red, or high-contrast neon. Don't use exclamation marks near grief content. Don't crowd the canvas. Never use stock photos of crying people. No dark humor. No urgency language ("Act NOW!", "Don't miss out!").

**Tone on social:** Same as the platform — gentle, honest, present tense. "You" and "we." Acknowledge pain without dwelling. Point toward light without minimizing darkness. Every post should pass the 2am test.

---

## Image Style

Soft watercolor-inspired illustrations in the LightPath palette. Figures should be abstract/silhouetted rather than detailed. Landscapes: lavender skies, golden paths, soft horizons. Prefer warm, misty atmospheres over sharp, clinical imagery. No stock photos of crying people. No urgency language near grief content.

# 6. RULES THAT NEVER BREAK

---

1. **Joy is earned, never assumed.** Sunrise tokens only appear after action or movement.
2. **Error is coral, never red.**
3. **Sage means progress.** Don't use decoratively on marketing — reserve for proof of real outcomes.
4. **Purple is the through-line.** The anchor that connects marketing and product.
5. **Dark mode = night sky.** Stars of light in darkness.
6. **The bloom mark is ❖.**
7. **2am test.** Every element — even the marketing site — must feel safe at someone's lowest moment.
8. **Sky gradient is sacred.** Lavender → sky → sage → mint.
9. **Section backgrounds are untouchable.** The Living Sky hero, cool/warm gradients, and dark sections are exactly as they are on the live website. Do not change them.
10. **Social media carries the same heart.** Every post, every story, every pixel passes the 2am test.
11. **Marketing is confident, not cold.** Professional warmth, not corporate distance.
12. **Lora carries the weight.** Never bold marketing headings — the serif carries enough authority at regular weight.
13. **Glassmorphism is the signature.** Frosted glass, not flat cards. Light, not heavy.
14. **The website is the first impression.** A grieving employee at 2am might find LightPath through their benefits page. The marketing site must carry them gently toward the product.
